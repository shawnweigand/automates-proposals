Redmax × AutoMates — Implementation Roadmap

Sequenced path from onboarding automation through full CRM + client portal
Foundation Quick Win Big Swing Nice to Have

How to read this roadmap

Six phases, sequenced so each one unlocks the next. Phase 1 stops the bleeding (finish what's already working). Phases 2–3 put a real system of record underneath the team so every downstream automation has somewhere to write to. Phases 4–5 are the big swings that depend on Monday being trustworthy. Phase 6 is the optional polish layer. Time ranges are planning estimates — not commitments.

Phase 1 · Weeks 1–2

Close the loop on what's already working

Foundation ~2 weeks Low risk

Goal: Finish the n8n onboarding flow that already took Keith's setup from 30 min → 2 min, and add QuickBooks as the final system it writes to. This is the single highest-leverage, lowest-risk ship.

QW-1

Finish onboarding buildout in n8n (+ QuickBooks)

Why first: The hard part is done. Adding the QuickBooks leg lets one trigger update Printavo, Dropbox, Gusto, QB, Trainual, CompanyCam, RE grid, and Calendar together — killing the misspelling/typo failure mode Brandon flagged.

Owner: AutoMates + Jenna Impact: high Effort: low
Why this is Phase 1: It's already 80% built and has proven time savings. Shipping it before anything else gives the team an early win, builds trust for the larger Monday buildout, and cleans up the static-field data so later phases aren't re-importing typos.
Phase 2 · Weeks 3–6

Stand up the Monday core + move comms to Slack

Quick Wins ~3–4 weeks Adoption-heavy

Goal: Replace the 15-tabs / Hot Sheet / group-text pattern with a single project board and a single comms surface. These are the foundation every later phase writes into.

QW-2

Centralize project management in Monday.com

Why now: Everything downstream — CRM, client portal, expense tagging, day-ahead digest — needs a single project board to hang off of. Start here.

Owner: AutoMates + Jenna Impact: high Effort: low–med
QW-3

Delegation + scheduling in Monday

Why now: Moves Keith off photo-texts and Jenna off email-driven scheduling. Pairs directly with QW-2 so tasks and calendars share the same board.

Owner: AutoMates + Keith + Jenna Depends on: QW-2
QW-4

Internal communication centralization (Slack)

Why now: One channel per project feeds into Monday via integrations later. Doing comms migration in parallel with the Monday core avoids a second disruption cycle.

Owner: AutoMates + Brandon Cultural investment
Why this is Phase 2: Monday PM and Slack are the two surfaces every other solution plugs into. Shipping them together (instead of sequentially) gets the change-management hit over once. Jenna's "15 tabs" problem and Keith's "walk-and-assign" problem both resolve here.
Phase 3 · Weeks 6–8

Start paying back the mental load

Quick Win ~2 weeks Quality of life

Goal: Jenna and Brandon stop starting the day with a mental inbox-scan. First automation layered on top of the new Monday + Slack foundation.

QW-5

Personalized day-ahead / week-ahead summary

Why now: Pulls from Monday, Calendar, and Slack — only possible after Phase 2. Directly answers Brandon's "magic wand = overall efficiency" and Jenna's "I don't know where to start" problem.

Owner: AutoMates Depends on: QW-2, QW-4 Impact: high
Why this is Phase 3: It's small but high-signal — an immediate daily payoff that reinforces adoption of the Monday and Slack investments.
Phase 4 · Weeks 8–16

Full Monday buildout — PM, CRM, and expenses

Big Swings ~8 weeks Core system

Goal: Monday becomes the actual source of truth for both pipeline and production. Expense data joins it so P&L-by-project stops being a manual typing exercise for Jenna.

BS-2

Monday PM buildout

Why now: The basic board from Phase 2 matures into the full lifecycle — drawings, assets, timeline, mid-project change lifecycle. Tavian stops chasing, Keith stops walking.

Owner: AutoMates + Jenna Depends on: QW-2, QW-3
BS-1

Monday CRM buildout

Why now: Pipeline lives in the Create@ inbox + Brandon's head today. With the PM side stable, pulling CRM into the same tool gives full lead-to-finish visibility without context-switching.

Owner: AutoMates + Brandon Depends on: BS-2
BS-3

Expense tracking system

Why now: The "select job" dropdown only works if the job list is trustworthy — that happens after BS-2. Replaces Jenna's second-shift receipt typing and fixes the Hello Frank tagging gap.

Owner: AutoMates + Jenna Depends on: BS-2
Why this is Phase 4: These three depend on Monday being real. Running them together lets expense/CRM/PM share the same data model instead of three mini-migrations later. This is the highest-effort block in the plan.
Phase 5 · Weeks 16–22

Client-facing layer + warehouse intelligence

Big Swings ~6 weeks External impact

Goal: With a trustworthy Monday underneath, surface the right parts to clients and start using computer vision on the shop floor.

BS-5

Client portal — drawings + updates synced with Monday

Why now: The portal is a Monday view. Build it only after BS-2 is real, so install dates, crew names, and the current-version-of-each-drawing can auto-populate instead of being typed in.

Owner: AutoMates + Brandon Depends on: BS-2 Highest external leverage
BS-4

Cameras + AI in warehouse for auto-reorder

Why now: Parallelizable with BS-5 — it lives in Keith's world, not Jenna's. Replaces the two-bin QR compliance step that Brandon admits nobody follows mid-rush. Human still approves the PO.

Owner: AutoMates + Keith Hardware + AI pilot
Why this is Phase 5: Both are high-impact but high-effort. They rely on the systems-of-record being stable, and they touch different parts of the org (client-facing vs. shop floor) so they can run in parallel with different leads.
Phase 6 · Weeks 22+

Polish — pitch decks, CAD sync, SMS cleanup

Nice to Haves Ongoing Opportunistic

Goal: Pick these up opportunistically. None are blockers for anything else; each one removes a persistent friction or unlocks scale in a specific function.

NH-1

Custom stylized AI pitch deck tool

Why here: Only valuable once outbound volume actually scales (100 pitches / 5 clients goal). Pairs with Monday CRM (BS-1) — decks only get built for warm leads.

Owner: AutoMates + John + Brandon Depends on: BS-1
NH-2

Fusion ↔ Rhino integration for Tavian

Why here: High personal impact (Tavian's 600+ min modeling per build) but isolated — doesn't unblock anyone else. Good candidate when a parallel stream is available.

Owner: AutoMates + Tavian Specialist scope
NH-3

SendBlue fix

Why here: Small footprint. Slack (QW-4) already absorbs most of the shared-channel use case, so this is only worth it if SMS-to-client volume stays meaningful.

Owner: AutoMates + Jenna Re-evaluate post-Slack
Why this is Phase 6: None of these block the rest of the roadmap. They're high-value in specific roles but not foundational. Revisit after Phase 5 based on which ones are still worth it.
