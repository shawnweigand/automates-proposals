Redmax × AutoMates — Before / After

Current-state pain points mapped one-to-one against the proposed solutions in the Opportunity Matrix. Sourced from interviews with Brandon, Jenna, Keith, and Tavian.

Quick Wins

Low effort, high impact — ship first
Quick Win QW-1 Finish Onboarding Buildout in n8n (+ QuickBooks)
Before
Same job data re-keyed into 8+ systems by a human, every time
  • Job name, number, dates, client name, and location manually entered into Dropbox, CompanyCam, Printavo, QuickBooks, expense tracker, RE grid, Google Calendar, Gusto, Trainual, handwritten shop sheets, and stickers/labels on client boxes.
  • Keith reports onboarding used to take ~30 minutes per job.
  • Jenna says the invoice name and job number alone get copy/pasted 7–8 times once a contract is signed.
  • A letter gets misspelled or a digit gets dropped and the wrong name ends up printed on a client-facing box.
Time/Cost~30 min per new job, multiplied across every job; ongoing error risk
SourcesBrandonKeithJenna
After
One trigger → every system updated, with QuickBooks closing the loop
  • n8n flow already live; cut Keith's onboarding from ~30 min to ~2 min.
  • Adding QuickBooks leg so a single trigger fires Printavo, Dropbox, Gusto, QuickBooks, Trainual, CompanyCam, RE grid, and Calendar together.
  • Zero re-entry for static fields, which kills the misspelling/typo failure mode Brandon called out.
Result~2 min per job; human error on static fields eliminated
Quick Win QW-2 Centralize Project Management in Monday.com
Before
Project state lives across 15 tabs, a Hot Sheet, and people's memory
  • Jenna: "My computer already has like 15 tabs open at a time."
  • RE grid exists as a tracker but isn't filled out — same for Printavo's built-in steps.
  • Progress tracking is verbal/visual; Tavian is usually the one chasing updates.
  • John (PM) produces a weekly Hot Sheet that gets emailed and posted on TVs — manual weekly rebuild.
  • Jenna: "If I miss a single day, I have so many emails... I don't even know where to start."
Time/CostDaily triangulation across Jenna, Keith, Tavian, and Brandon; tracking info duplicated or missing
SourcesJennaTavianBrandon
After
One lightweight project board everyone sees and updates
  • Single Monday project board replacing the 15-tabs + Hot Sheet pattern.
  • Becomes the foundation the larger Monday buildout plugs into.
  • Kills the "is the RE grid filled out?" question — there's one place.
ResultVisibility replaces triangulation; meetings stop starting with "where are we on X?"
Quick Win QW-3 Delegation + Scheduling in Monday.com
Before
Scheduling runs on Google Calendar + text threads + verbal handoffs
  • Main Redmax Google Calendar is the source for staffing; personal calendars are separate.
  • Gusto time-off sync works, but weekend unavailability and shift changes are emailed in.
  • Warehouse delegation is ad-hoc: Keith walks the shop, takes photos on his phone, and texts three guys in a group chat who "like" the photo when done.
  • Keith: "I have to go back there and write it all down... there's wasted time on my end because I have to go to them and show them what to do."
  • Keith and Jenna co-own scheduling; owners, due dates, and status are not consistently captured in one place.
Time/CostKeith pulled off his own work to delegate; installer/crew status known only by whoever asked last
SourcesKeithJennaBrandon
After
Tasks, owners, due dates, and status all live in Monday
  • Installer/crew scheduling and task delegation moves out of Calendar + texts into Monday.
  • Keith, Jenna, and Brandon stop triangulating — they read one board.
  • Warehouse "this needs to happen today" list can be assigned ahead instead of ad-hoc photo texts.
ResultDelegation happens once, in writing; Keith reclaims time currently spent walking and assigning
Quick Win QW-4 Internal Communication Centralization (Slack)
Before
"Was it a text, a call, or an email?" — comms are everywhere
  • Every job gets a group chat (Brandon, Keith, Jenna, Tavian, Fred, + installers).
  • Clients jump between email, text, and phone calls; notes from calls don't get written down.
  • Jenna: "Brandon will take a call from that client... no one knows what was said in that call."
  • SendBlue (shared texting number) is paid for but "doesn't really work great" — can't reliably send attachments or add contacts, so team defaults back to personal numbers.
  • Keith flags the same problem on the build side — print department vs. warehouse vs. shop use different names for the same asset, so context gets lost.
Time/CostInformation siloed per person; critical client approvals (e.g., $10K add-ons) happen over text with no paper trail
SourcesJennaKeithBrandon
After
One channel per project, mobile-friendly, everyone included
  • Slack culture is built intentionally — investment in training and adoption.
  • Ends the "where was that conversation?" problem and gives one clean surface to feed into Monday later via integrations.
  • Group chats, SendBlue fragments, and scattered emails collapse into one thread per project.
ResultContext stops living in one person's brain; onboarding a new PM doesn't require a memory dump

Every morning starts with a mental-load scan across inboxes and threads
  • Jenna pretty much in every email thread the company sees; a single missed day is overwhelming.

  • Brandon's "magic wand": overall efficiency — cut hours worked per project.
Time/CostMental-load tax at the start of every day, especially after any day off
SourcesJennaBrandon

Automated morning brief pulled from Monday, Calendar, and Slack
  • Daily/weekly digest for Jenna and Brandon — today's priorities, this week's deliverables, flagged risks, open approvals.

ResultRe-orients the day in ~2 minutes; no "where do I start" after a day off

Big Swings

High effort, high impact — restructure how the business runs
Big Swing BS-1 Monday.com CRM Buildout
Before
No pipeline — leads live in the Create@ inbox and Brandon's head
  • Inbound from referrals + agencies lands in the Create@ inbox; Jenna and Brandon sit on that address.
  • Brandon handles quotes on projects $50K+ (~80% of all projects); Jenna handles <$50K.
  • Pitch/outreach pipeline (John's decks) is tracked informally — ~6 sent, goal of 100 to land 5 meaningful clients.
  • No systematic view of "what's in the pipeline, what stage, what's likely to close."
Time/CostNo forecasting, no conversion visibility; proactive client outreach is ad-hoc
SourcesBrandonJenna
After
Full lead-to-finish lifecycle owned in Monday
  • Inbound leads → qualification → quote → won/lost → handoff to production all in one board.
  • Replaces the Create@ inbox scramble.
  • Gives Brandon + Jenna real pipeline visibility and a place to track pitch decks, follow-ups, and repeat-client cadence.
ResultPipeline becomes a dashboard instead of a scavenger hunt
Big Swing BS-2 Monday.com Project Management Buildout
Before
Every project's source of truth is fragmented across 8+ tools
  • Drawings in Dropbox, invoices in QuickBooks, prints in Printavo, pack lists in CompanyCam, schedule in Google Calendar, KPIs in Google Sheets, SOPs in Trainual, photos in CompanyCam.
  • Mid-project changes ("client upgraded the vinyl logo to dimensional") have no standardized lifecycle — Brandon: "Where does that add go? Who knows about it? Is it on the invoice? Is it on the pack list?"
  • Tavian spends significant time chasing who knows what on each build (85 emails/day on drawing changes).
  • Keith has to walk to the print room to find out what graphics are going on a wall — labels in Printavo don't match warehouse labels.
Time/CostDuplicated conversations, missed add-ons, PM context only reconstructable by interrogating four people
SourcesBrandonTavianKeithJenna
After
One board per project — the single source of truth
  • Tasks, owners, drawings, assets, timeline, and status all on the project board.
  • Kills the "15 tabs + Hot Sheet + Slack" pattern.
  • Mid-project changes have a defined home: they flow to invoice, pack list, and assignee automatically.
ResultTavian, Keith, and Jenna stop repeating context; $10K add-ons stop living only in a text thread
Big Swing BS-3 Expense Tracking System
Before
Manual receipt entry with per-job attribution done by hand
  • 4 named AMEX cards (Brandon, Jenna, Tavian, Keith) + 3 gas cards in a lockbox.
  • Expenses manually entered into a Google Sheets expense tracker.
  • The tracker is NOT just for categorization — it's for job attribution: which specific job does each expense belong to.
  • A single AMEX charge could be split: e.g., $6,000 material order = $3K for Job A + $3K for Job B.
  • Jenna also adds job time to the sheet by hand after the fact.
  • Brandon trialed Hello Frank: loves the UI but there's no way to tag a transaction to a job.
Time/CostHigh daily data-entry load on Jenna; P&L-by-project only as accurate as the last person who entered a receipt
SourcesBrandonJenna
After
Transactions captured, categorized, and job-tagged automatically
  • AMEX feed lands in one place; each line gets a "select job" dropdown for tagging.
  • Per-job P&L stays clean with minimal human data entry.
  • Double-entry between card statements and the expense sheet disappears.
ResultCuts the "second-shift" expense typing Jenna currently does; accuracy improves on gross margin per job
Big Swing BS-4 Cameras + AI in Warehouse for Auto-Reorder
Before
Consumable restocks rely on a two-bin QR scan that doesn't get done
  • Keith built a green/red two-bin system sized to ~1 week of supply for each consumable.
  • When red side shows, worker is supposed to flip the bin, scan a QR, and fill out a Google Form — which triggers an email to reorder.
  • Brandon: "If I'm rushing to grab a roll of tape to throw in the back of the truck... am I turning that bin around, scanning a QR code, filling out a Google form?"
  • Keith adds new items to the system manually — build a box, paint it, measure the product, copy a Google Form template, paste SKU from Home Depot, add a photo.
  • Result: stockouts mid-build (e.g., "we're out of screws") are a weekly failure mode.
Time/CostStockouts interrupt builds; Keith spends hours creating forms + physical bins for every new SKU
SourcesBrandonKeith
After
Camera + AI watches the aisle and flags the human to confirm reorder
  • Security camera on the consumables aisle (cameras already throughout the building).
  • AI detects a red bin that stays red for 5+ minutes and alerts Keith/purchasing with a screenshot.
  • No auto-order — human approves before the PO goes out, preventing false triggers.
  • Brandon: "You said nothing's impossible, so I'll push the list."
ResultRemoves a physical-compliance step from the shop; stockout-during-build mostly disappears
Big Swing BS-5 Client Portal — Drawings + Updates Synced with Monday
Before
Approvals happen in long email chains with attachments and Dropbox links
  • Shop drawings and graphic approvals both flow over email; clients routinely approve the wrong version.
  • Old versions in Dropbox, but clients don't have clean access — they grab an old attachment and approve it.
  • Ashore worked for print approvals, but required double-entry (fill out Ashore + email the link anyway) — got dropped.
  • 17 Hats had a client portal with invoices/quotes but no approvals; Core Bridge has approvals but is print/apparel-shaped — nothing on the market fits.
  • Crew names for venue security lists, vehicle/truck info, old quotes/invoices, W9, wire info — all sent ad-hoc on request.
  • $10K add-ons sometimes approved over text; no paper trail.
Time/CostVersion confusion, re-sends, version-4-mistakenly-reviewed scenarios; admin chases client approvals constantly
SourcesBrandonJenna
After
Custom client portal pulling directly from Monday
  • Separate sections for shop drawings vs. graphic approvals; most-current version prominent, older versions gated to prevent confusion.
  • Job details (install date, location, crew names, vehicle, logistics) auto-populate from Monday.
  • Billing tab: view quotes, current invoice, pay in-portal, access W9/wire info.
  • Communication stays on email/text — portal captures the text-approval screenshot for paper trail.
  • Approve/reject directly from the portal.
ResultKills the email back-and-forth on revisions; clients see what matters without Redmax re-sending it

SourcesBrandon

Templated generator that pulls in brand research and past work
  • Spin up an on-brand pitch deck fast; John + Brandon feed the prospect, tool does the first pass.

  • Solves Brandon's "pitch deck pain" without making him become a designer.
ResultDeck prep time drops meaningfully per pitch; volume scales without a second creative hire

  • Tavian models structure in Rhino 3D, then post-processes in Fusion for manufacturing layers/nests/simulation.

Time/CostMaterial re-sync time on every build; blocks Tavian from moving into a supervisory role
SourcesTavian

Automated parameter / geometry export between Rhino and Fusion
  • Even partial automation — parameter passing or geometry export — shaves time off every job.
  • Removes the consistent friction point that makes outsourcing drafting harder.
  • Tavian can focus supervisory + KPI time instead of rebuilding models.
ResultRecoups a meaningful slice of the 600+ min modeling time per build

Cross-Cutting Themes

Patterns that show up across multiple opportunities — useful as framing for the roadmap narrative.
Redundant data entry
Brandon's magic-wand answer and his closing ask — same info in 8+ places, entered by a human every time. Shows up in onboarding, project management, expense tracking, and client approvals.
Context trapped in people's heads
Jenna explicitly called out the mental-load problem; Tavian asked her in February to write down "anything you just have in your brain" before leaving. Same pattern for Keith's warehouse delegation.
Tools exist but don't get used
RE grid, Printavo steps, CompanyCam, Trainual, Ashore — all solve real problems on paper but fail in practice because of friction, double-entry, or shop-floor adoption barriers.
Shop-floor vs. office adoption gap
Office team is young, eager for AI and polished tools. Shop team's hands are covered in paint and dust — tech needs to push info TO them, not ask them to input it.
Mid-project changes have no lifecycle
Client add-ons live in email chains, group texts, or memory — not on the invoice, pack list, or schedule until someone remembers to push them there.
